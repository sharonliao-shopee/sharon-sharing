Quick Start for New

Start with one small task from your real work and build your own way of working with Codex

You do not need to learn every feature at once. Connect the systems you use most, deliver something useful, and keep what works.

1

Install essential plugins

Connect your everyday documents, email, and calendar so Codex can read real work materials within your permissions.

2

Start with a simple task

Choose real work already due this week with an easy-to-judge result, such as a brief, option comparison, or action list.

3

Give clear instructions

Define the context, objective, audience, sources, requirements, and boundaries. Confirm the brief, review a first draft, follow up, then finalize.

4

PAGE 3-2 · SMALL TASKS
Quick Start · Step 2

Start with a simple task: Choose a real task already due this week, with an outcome that is easy to evaluate.

Complexity matters less than real source material, a clear outcome, and your ability to judge whether the result helps.

A

One-page project brief

See the project context, progress, risks, and decisions needed at a glance.

Review @Project Materials and summarize: 1. Problem to solve 2. Current progress and decisions 3. Risks, dependencies, and gaps 4. Critical actions for the next two weeks 5. Decisions I need to make Every number and conclusion must trace back to a source.
B

Option comparison

Compare options on a common basis and make the assumptions behind the recommendation explicit.

Review @Project A and @Project B. Define a common framework, then compare: • Benefits and costs • Implementation difficulty • Risks and prerequisites Close with your recommendation, assumptions, information gaps, and what the pilot should validate.
C

Meeting action list

Turn meeting notes into decisions, actions, PICs, and due dates.

Review @Meeting Transcript and extract: • Confirmed decisions • Unresolved disagreements • Action / PIC / Due date • Missing data or materials • Items due before the next meeting If no PIC or date is explicit, mark it “To confirm.”
◆Choose:Already due this week + real sources + an easy-to-judge result + an output you can use now.

PAGE 3-3 · CLEAR INSTRUCTION
Quick Start · Step 3

Give clear instructions: Define the context, delivery standard, and boundaries

Brief Codex as you would a new colleague: the clearer the context, the more usable the result.

Context

Why this is happening

Objective

What needs to be solved

Audience

Who will read and decide

Sources

What Codex should review

Requirements

Length, structure, format

Boundaries

What not to assume

Full example | Executive project update Context: We want to propose a new project to leadership, but the supporting materials are scattered. Objective: Help leaders understand in 10 minutes why we should do it, the proposal, and the support needed. Sources: Review the project brief, research notes, and available data. Requirements: Start with a storyline of no more than five slides. Use a conclusion-led title on each slide. Build the full content after I confirm it. Boundaries: Do not invent data. Flag anything unverified. Ask before editing the source file or sending anything externally. When complete, review it from a leader’s perspective: Which claims lack evidence, and which questions are most likely to come up?
◆How to collaborate:Give clear instructions, but do not try to make the first prompt perfect.
